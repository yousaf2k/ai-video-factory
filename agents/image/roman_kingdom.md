## Historical Context & Accuracy

### Time Period
- **Focus**: Roman Kingdom (Regal Period) - 753-509 BCE
- **Seven Kings**: Romulus, Numa Pompilius, Tullus Hostilius, Ancus Marcius, Tarquinius Priscus, Servius Tullius, Tarquinius Superbus
- **Key Feature**: This was **early, developing Rome** - not the imperial marble city of later centuries
- **Cultural Context**: Heavy Etruscan influence, especially in later kingdom period

### Architecture & Urban Development

**Early Settlement Characteristics:**
- **Hut dwellings**: Early Romans lived in simple oval/rectangular huts with thatched roofs
- **Wattle and daub**: Walls made of woven branches covered with clay
- **Thatched roofs**: Straw roofs, later transitioning to terracotta tiles under Etruscan influence
- **Palatine Hill**: Original settlement area with primitive dwellings
- **Roman Forum**: Originally marshy ground, drained and paved (Cloaca Maxima built)
- **Capitoline Hill**: Early fortifications and temple sites

**Building Materials:**
- **Timber**: Oak and pine for structural frames
- **Mud brick**: Sun-dried bricks for walls
- **Tufa and cappellaccio**: Local volcanic stone for foundations
- **Terracotta**: Roof tiles, architectural decorations (Etruscan influence)
- **Thatch**: Straw roofs for simpler dwellings
- **Wood**: Columned porches, door frames, shutters

**Public Buildings:**
- **Temple of Jupiter Optimus Maximus**: Built on Capitoline Hill (started under Tarquinius Priscus)
- **Regia**: Original king's residence/office in Forum
- **Curia Hostilia**: Early senate house (simple structure)
- **Cloaca Maxima**: Great drain to swampy Forum area
- **Circus Maximus**: Early chariot racing venue (simple embankments)
- **Servian Wall**: Early fortification wall (earliest version)
- **Sacred way (Via Sacra**: Processional route through Forum

**No Classical Roman Features Yet:**
- ❌ No marble temples or buildings
- ❌ No concrete (opus caementicium - developed later)
- ❌ No monumental arches or columns in classical style
- ❌ No imperial fora or basilicas
- ❌ No Colosseum, Pantheon, or later monuments

### Clothing & Appearance

**Men's Attire:**
- **Toga**: Early form of toga, simpler than later versions, worn by citizens
- **Tunica**: Simple knee-length tunic worn under toga or alone by workers
- **Belt**: Leather belts or cords at waist
- **Footwear**: Leather sandals (calcei) or sturdy boots for outdoors, barefoot indoors
- **Headwear**: Simple brimless hats (pileus) or bare head
- **Undergarments**: Subligaculum (loincloth)

**Women's Attire:**
- **Stola**: Long dress worn by married women (early form)
- **Tunica**: Long tunic reaching ankles
- **Palla**: Shawl or mantle draped over head and body
- **Belt**: Corded sashes at waist
- **Footwear**: Sandals (calcei) or soft leather shoes
- **Jewelry**: Fibulae (brooches), bracelets, necklaces, earrings in bronze, silver, or gold
- **Hairstyles**: Long hair, often bound or covered with palla

**Military Dress (Early Army):**
- **Phalanx formation**: Greek-style hoplite warfare (early period)
- **Body armor**: Bronze breastplates (muscle cuirass style) for wealthy
- **Helmets**: Bronze Corinthian-style or Italic bronze helmets with crests
- **Shields**: Round bronze shields (aspis/clipeus style)
- **Weapons**: Spears (hasta), swords (early gladius variants)
- **Greaves**: Bronze shin guards for officers/wealthy
- **No lorica segmentata**: That's much later imperial period
- **No standardized equipment**: Varied by individual wealth

**Royal Dress:**
- **Toga praetexta**: Toga with purple border worn by magistrates and king
- **Trabea**: Purple toga for special ceremonies
- **Scepter**: Ivory staff with eagle topping
- **Curule chair**: Folding ivory seat for magistrates

### Material Culture & Objects

**Everyday Items:**
- **Pottery**: Impasto ware (brown, handmade), later bucchero (Etruscan influence)
- **Tools**: Iron and bronze agricultural tools, carpentry tools
- **Lamps**: Simple clay oil lamps (later bronze)
- **Fibulae**: Safety-pin style brooches for fastening garments
- **Mirrors**: Polished bronze mirrors
- **Weights and measures**: Bronze and lead weights
- **Coins**: No coins yet - used aes rude (rough bronze pieces) for exchange

**Weapons and Armor:**
- **Swords**: Early bronze/iron swords, Greek xiphos influence
- **Spears**: Bronze-tipped spears (hasta) for infantry
- **Javelins**: Throwing spears
- **Shields**: Round bronze-faced shields (aspis) and oval leather/clad shields
- **Armor**: Bronze breastplates, linen corselets, leather jerkins
- **Helmets**: Bronze Corinthian, Italic, and attic styles

**Religious Objects:**
- **Altars**: Simple stone altars for sacrifices
- **Temples**: Etruscan-style temples with high podiums and deep porches
- **Statues**: Terracotta statues (Apollo of Veii style), early bronze cult statues
- **Household shrines**: Lararium (household altar for household gods)

### Social Structure & Activities

**Social Classes:**
- **Patricians**: Aristocratic families, originally clan elders
- **Clients**: Dependent followers of patricians
- **Plebeians**: Common free citizens
- **Slaves**: Captured in war or born into slavery

**Daily Activities:**
- **Agriculture**: Farming, shepherding, viticulture (wine growing)
- **Trade**: Market at Forum, trading with Greeks and Etruscans
- **Craft production**: Pottery, metalwork, weaving
- **Military training**: Drills on Campus Martius (Field of Mars)
- **Political assemblies**: Meetings in Comitium
- **Religious ceremonies**: Sacrifices, festivals, processions

### Animals & Agriculture

**Domesticated Animals:**
- **Cattle**: Oxen for plowing, cows for dairy and meat
- **Sheep and goats**: For wool, milk, and meat
- **Pigs**: Common food source
- **Horses**: For cavalry and chariots (wealthy)
- **Dogs**: Guard dogs, hunting dogs
- **Chickens and geese**: Food and eggs

**Crops:**
- **Grains**: Wheat, barley, spelt (far)
- **Olives**: For oil
- **Grapes**: For wine
- **Vegetables**: Beans, lentils, cabbage
- **Figs**: Important fruit crop

## Video & Motion Prompt Guidelines (For Wan 2.2 I2V)

The `motion_prompt` must NOT just be camera directions. It must describe a living, moving scene to guide the video generation model.

When writing the `motion_prompt`, follow these strict rules:

1. **Summarize the Scene:** Briefly state what is in the shot (e.g., "A view of early Roman huts on Palatine Hill...", "The Forum market scene...").
2. **Subject Motion (Verbs):** Describe exactly how the people/animals are moving. Use dynamic verbs (e.g., "Romans walk through the Forum," "Workers carry timber," "Etruscan architects direct construction").
3. **Environmental Motion:** Describe how the elements react (e.g., "Smoke rises from cooking fires," "Dust swirls from construction," "Fabric flutter in the breeze").
4. **Camera Movement:** End with the camera instruction (e.g., "The camera slowly pans across the scene," "Static camera capturing daily life").
5. **NO PHOTOGRAPHY TERMS:** You MUST strip out all static photography terms from the motion prompt. Do NOT include terms like "8K", "50mm", "f/8", "DSLR", "hyper-detailed", "sharp focus", or "ISO" in the motion prompt. Use cinematic pacing terms instead ("cinematic pacing," "fluid motion," "natural movement").

**Example structure:**
```
"[Scene context with people/animals] + [Specific dynamic movements of subjects] + [Environmental effects like fire/wind] + [Camera movement]. Cinematic pacing, realistic physics, fluid natural motion."
```
