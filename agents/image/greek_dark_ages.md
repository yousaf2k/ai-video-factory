## Historical Context & Accuracy

### Time Period
- **Focus**: Greek Dark Ages - c. 1100-800 BCE
- **Key Transition**: Late Bronze Age collapse to Early Iron Age recovery
- **Stages**:
  - Early (1100-1050 BCE): Collapse, destruction, abandonments
  - Middle (1050-900 BCE): Lowest point, small settlements, Proto-Geometric pottery
  - Late (900-800 BCE): Recovery, population growth, Geometric pottery, rise of polis

### Architecture & Settlements

**Settlement Characteristics:**
- **Drastically reduced**: Small villages and hamlets, not cities
- **Hilltop locations**: For defense and refuge (acropoleis)
- **Abandoned palaces**: Mycenaean palaces destroyed and abandoned
- **Simple dwellings**: Small one- or two-room houses with wattle-and-daub walls
- **Thatched roofs**: Straw or reed thatching
- **Circuit walls**: Some sites refortified with cyclopean walls (Tiryns, Athens)
- **No monumental architecture**: No palaces, no grand temples, no large public buildings

**Building Materials:**
- **Mud brick**: Sun-dried bricks for walls
- **Stone foundations**: Field stones, rubble
- **Timber**: Wooden posts, beams, roof frames
- **Thatch**: Straw, reeds for roofing
- **Clay/plaster**: Mud plaster for walls
- **Terracotta**: Roof tiles emerge later (end of period)

**Site Types:**
- **Refuge settlements**: Hilltop sites for defense
- **Small villages**: Cluster of houses around a central area
- **Isolated farmsteads**: Single-family dwellings in countryside
- **Reoccupied Mycenaean sites**: People living among ruins of palaces (Athens, Tiryns)
- **Burial sites**: Cemeteries with tumuli, cist graves, pit graves

### Clothing & Appearance

**Men's Attire:**
- **Chiton**: Simple rectangular cloth pinned at shoulders (early form)
- **Himation**: Rectangular cloak wrapped around body
- **Doric peplos**: Heavy woolen rectangle folded and pinned
- **Belts**: Leather or woven cords
- **Footwear**: Leather sandals (calcei), simple boots, or barefoot
- **Headwear**: Petasos (wide-brimmed felt hat) for travelers, or bare head
- **Jewelry**: Simple bronze pins, fibulae, armbands

**Women's Attire:**
- **Peplos**: Long woolen rectangle folded and pinned at shoulders
- **Chiton**: Lighter linen tunic (less common in Dark Ages)
- **Himation**: Outer cloak or shawl
- **Belts/Sashes**: Corded belts at waist
- **Fibulae**: Safety-pin style brooches for fastening
- **Jewelry**: Bronze bracelets, necklaces, earrings, finger rings
- **Hairstyles**: Long hair bound or covered with cloth

**Warrior Dress (Early Dark Ages):**
- **Body armor**: Rare - linen or leather corselets for wealthy
- **Helmets**: Bronze conical helmets, sometimes with horsehair crests
- **Shields**: Round bronze shields (aspis) figure-8 shields (early period)
- **Weapons**: Iron swords replacing bronze, spears (hasta), daggers
- **Greaves**: Bronze shin guards (rare, for wealthy warriors)
- **No organized armies**: Individual warriors, not professional soldiers

### Material Culture & Objects

**Pottery (Key Marker):**
- **Sub-Mycenaean (1100-1050 BCE):** Decline in quality, simplified shapes
- **Proto-Geometric (1050-900 BCE):** Simple geometric motifs, circles, semicircles
- **Geometric (900-700 BCE):** Meander patterns, triangles, concentric circles, human figures
- **Technique**: Wheel-turned, improved firing, dark on light decoration

**Weapons and Tools:**
- **Iron working**: New technology replacing bronze (small items first)
- **Swords**: Iron swords with leaf-shaped blades
- **Spears**: Iron spearheads (cheaper than bronze)
- **Knives**: Utility knives, daggers
- **Tools**: Iron axes, chisels, sickles, plow points
- **Ritual objects**: Bronze figurines, tripods, cauldrons

**Burial Practices:**
- **Cist graves**: Rectangular pits lined with stone slabs
- **Pit graves**: Simple shaft graves
- **Tumuli**: Burial mounds for elite (Lefkandi)
- **Cremation**: Increasingly common (especially in Athens)
- **Grave goods**: Pottery, weapons, jewelry, figurines

**Everyday Items:**
- **Loom weights**: Terracotta weights for weaving
- **Spindle whorls**: For spinning wool
- **Cooking pots**: Bronze cauldrons, clay cooking pots
- **Storage jars**: Large pithoi for grain and oil
- **Oil lamps**: Simple clay lamps
- **Figurines**: Terracotta human and animal figures

### Social Structure & Activities

**Social Organization:**
- **Basileis**: Local chieftains or "kings" (warrior leaders)
- **Tribal groups**: Organized by tribes and clans (phylai, genē)
- **Reduced complexity**: No centralized state, no palace bureaucracy
- **Oral tradition**: No writing (Linear B lost), Homer's epics preserve oral culture
- **Ancestor worship**: Hero cults at Mycenaean tombs

**Daily Activities:**
- **Agriculture**: Wheat, barley, olives, grapes, figs
- **Pastoralism**: Sheep, goats, cattle (transhumance)
- **Craft production**: Pottery, metalworking, weaving
- **Trade**: Limited but not eliminated - luxury goods from Cyprus, Levant
- **Warfare**: Local conflicts, raiding, warrior culture
- **Religious rituals**: Sacrifices, libations, offerings at shrines

### Animals & Agriculture

**Domesticated Animals:**
- **Sheep and goats**: Primary livestock (wool, meat, milk)
- **Cattle**: Oxen for plowing, meat
- **Pigs**: Food source
- **Horses**: For elite warriors (chariots in early period, riding later)
- **Dogs**: Guard dogs, hunting dogs

**Crops:**
- **Grains**: Wheat, barley, millet
- **Olives**: For oil
- **Grapes**: For wine (production declines then recovers)
- **Figs**: Important fruit
- **Legumes**: Beans, lentils, peas

**Avoid Anachronisms:**
- ❌ No classical Greek temples (Parthenon, etc. - that's 5th century BCE)
- ❌ No marble sculpture (kouroi, korai - that's Archaic period)

**Include Accurate Elements:**
- ✅ Simple hut dwellings with thatched roofs
- ✅ Abandoned Mycenaean palaces in ruins
- ✅ Proto-Geometric and Geometric pottery
- ✅ Iron weapons replacing bronze

## Video & Motion Prompt Guidelines (For Wan 2.2 I2V)

The `motion_prompt` must NOT just be camera directions. It must describe a living, moving scene to guide the video generation model.

When writing the `motion_prompt`, follow these strict rules:

1. **Summarize the Scene:** Briefly state what is in the shot (e.g., "A village scene in post-collapse Greece...", "Warriors gathering at a refuge settlement...").
2. **Subject Motion (Verbs):** Describe exactly how the people/animals are moving. Use dynamic verbs (e.g., "Warriors walk slowly through the ruins," "Women weave at ground-level looms," "Potter shapes clay on wheel").
3. **Environmental Motion:** Describe how the elements react (e.g., "Smoke rises from cooking fires," "Wind rustles through thatched roofs," "Dust swirls in abandoned spaces").
4. **Camera Movement:** End with the camera instruction (e.g., "The camera slowly pans across the scene," "Static camera capturing quiet moments").
5. **NO PHOTOGRAPHY TERMS:** You MUST strip out all static photography terms from the motion prompt. Do NOT include terms like "8K", "50mm", "f/8", "DSLR", "hyper-detailed", "sharp focus", or "ISO" in the motion prompt. Use cinematic pacing terms instead ("cinematic pacing," "fluid motion," "natural movement").

**Example structure:**
```
"[Scene context with people/animals] + [Specific dynamic movements of subjects] + [Environmental effects like smoke/wind] + [Camera movement]. Cinematic pacing, realistic physics, fluid natural motion."
```
